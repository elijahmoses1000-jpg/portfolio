01 — About

A builder at the intersection of education, technology, and opportunity.

For the past nine years I've been obsessed with one question: why does education in Africa fail to translate into employment? The continent has the world's youngest, fastest-growing population — yet over 60% of graduates remain underemployed. The system isn't broken; it's just optimized for the wrong century.

I've spent that decade building toward an answer — as Head of Partnership at RLabs Nigeria running diagnostics for 45 startups; co-founding Zander Tech to a $50K Markhack 2.0 grant; running growth at Metafluence from 41,000 to 80,000 community in 13 days; and as CMO at Kugatel generating $121K in sales. Across roles I've raised $60K, executed $250K+ in grant-funded projects, and trained 80+ startup founders.

What I'm best at: building intelligent systems with AI and end-to-end automation. I've shipped 40+ AI products — most recently ZeusX (an elite prompt workspace for Claude) and OmegaX (an AI-powered healthcare intelligence platform for West Africa) — and built VerionX, where students learn, earn, and get hired in one continuous loop. I'm also a World Literacy Foundation Ambassador for Nigeria, an Aspire Leaders Fellow, and a Lagos Business School ViMP alum.

I'm on a mission to drive digital transformation for 100 million people across Africa by 2050, and to contribute to a collective effort to build 2,000,000 ventures across the world. Inspired by Idealab — and convinced the model can be expanded for emerging markets. I also write at medium.com/@ebibliophile45; my recent essay: The Native Intelligence: Governing AI for the Nigerian Good. A Doctorate in Innovation & Value Creation is also in view.
